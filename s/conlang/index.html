BLAH
a constructed language

Introduction

BLAH is a conlang. It was created for no real purpose, other than out of curiosity/interest.

Phonology and orthography

The following is a list of all sounds present in the BLAH language:

Vowels
Front a ɛ i

Graphemes are written exactly identically to their IPA counterparts, except for the following:

  • The glottal stop may also be written as ? or Ɂ. The ? form is recommended.
  • The implosives and ejectives are written as their pulmonic counterparts followed by an apostrophe (').
  • The open-mid front unrounded vowel [ɛ] is written as e.

Hence, the BLAH alphabet is pbtdcɟkg?fvθðszʃʒçʝxɣhʘ|!'aeiou, with a répertoire of 28 consonants, 5 vowels, 2 vowel separators, and 3 clicks. An "alphabetical order" has not yet been devised.

In spoken BLAH, at the whim of the speaker:

  • Velar consonants may be substitued for uvular consonants.
  • Clicks may be pronounced prenasalized. It is recommended to use the voiced bilabial nasal for the bilabial click, /m͡ʘ/, the voiced dental nasal for the dental click, /n̪͡!/, and the voiced alveolar nasal for the alveolar click, /n͡|/.

Other notes about BLAH phonology/orthography:

  • Retroflex, uvular, and pharyngeal consonants were omitted on the grounds of either being too similar to existing sounds or too difficult to pronounce.
  • The bilabial fricatives were omitted to create symmetry with the lack of labiodental plosives.
  • All glottal sounds can only be used between vowels.
